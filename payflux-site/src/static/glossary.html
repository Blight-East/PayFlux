Glossary

Essential terminology for payment processing and risk management used throughout the PayFlux platform.

Acquirer

A financial institution that processes payment card transactions on behalf of merchants. The acquirer underwrites the merchant and assumes liability for chargebacks.

BIN (Bank Identification Number)

The first 6–8 digits of a payment card that identify the issuing bank, card type, and country of origin. Used in risk analysis for geographic and issuer-based scoring.

Chargeback

A reversal of a payment transaction initiated by the cardholder or issuing bank. High chargeback ratios trigger processor scrutiny and can lead to account termination.

CVV (Card Verification Value)

A security code on payment cards used to verify card-not-present transactions. Absence of CVV in transactions is a significant risk signal.

Evidence Narrative

A structured explanation generated by PayFlux that provides context for risk signals, designed to be shared with processors during remediation conversations.

False Positive

A legitimate transaction incorrectly flagged as fraudulent by risk detection systems. Reducing false positives is critical for maintaining approval rates and customer experience.

Fraud Rate

The percentage of transactions determined to be fraudulent within a given time period. Processor risk models typically flag merchants exceeding 0.9% fraud-to-sales ratios.

Gateway

A payment processing platform that connects merchants to payment networks and acquirers, handling authorization, capture, and settlement of transactions.

Interchange

Fees charged by card networks and issuing banks for processing payment transactions. Interchange rates vary by card type, merchant category, and transaction method.

Issuer

A financial institution that issues payment cards to consumers. Issuers make authorization decisions and bear fraud liability for card-present transactions.

MCC (Merchant Category Code)

A four-digit code assigned to merchants by payment processors that classifies the type of business. MCC codes influence interchange rates and risk scoring.

PCI DSS

Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard — a set of security requirements for organizations that handle payment card data. Compliance is mandatory for all merchants.

Risk Band

A categorical classification (low, elevated, high, critical) assigned to risk scores for simplified decisioning and alert routing.

Risk Score

A numerical value (typically 0.0–1.0) indicating the likelihood that a transaction or pattern is fraudulent or high-risk. PayFlux uses deterministic scoring for full auditability.

Settlement

The final transfer of funds from acquirer to merchant for completed transactions, typically occurring 1–3 business days after authorization.

Velocity

The rate or frequency of transactions from a single source within a defined time period. Velocity checks are a fundamental component of fraud prevention systems.
